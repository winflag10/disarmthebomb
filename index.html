Welcome to DisarmTheBomb.com! A game made by John Watkins about, you guessed it... disarming bombs. This was made as a learning project for html, javascript and css and was inspired by the popular game keep talking and nobody explodes. Check it out HERE.

You will be presented with a randomly generated bomb comprised of individually solvable modules. In order to defuse the bomb all modules must be defused before all lives are lost or before the timer reaches 0. If a mistake is made, you will lose a life (Indicated by the red crosses at the top of the bomb) and the timer will begin to count down faster.

Every module is indicated with a grey box, as seen to the left. They are all solved differently and instructions for each are located in the Bomb Defusal Manual. Ensure this is up to date by checking the version number on the manual matches the one displayed on the main menu.

Single Player
(not recommended)

You act as both the defuser and the intelligence. You can see both the bomb and the manual and aim to defuse the bomb as fast as you can. CHALLENGE: Try and memorise the manual and defuse from memory.

2+ Players
(recommended)

One player is the defuser and can see only the bomb. All other players are intelligence and can see just the manual. You must work together as a bomb squad to defuse the bomb as fast as you can.
